The pattern field

The pattern field consists of the avaliable channels. Each channel has four columns.
The first column defines what note and octave to play, the second defines the instrument used, the third sets volume and the last one is used for channel effects.

Channel info

It's possible to disable channels by clicking its name on top of pattern.

Square 1 & 2

Produces square waveforms. 
They cannot produce sound below A-0 (G-0 for PAL). Both channels are identical.

Triangle

Produces a triangle-formed waveform. It isn't possible to set volume, so the volume column have no effect on this channel.
It's one octave lower than the square channels and cannot produce sound below A-0 (G-0 for PAL).

Noise

Produces white-noise sound.
It's only possible to play 16 different frequencies, where the lowest frequency is played at C-0 and highest at D#1 (going out of that range will wrap).

DPCM

This one plays samples that are loaded from files. Instrument effects are not used at all.
It is only able to play samples at 16 different pitches, defined in the instrument settings (DPCM samples-tab).
Notes above octave 5 will cause samples to loop.

Channel effects

Effects are entered in the last column, in form of Xxx. (X - Effect number, xx - Effect parameter)

 

Axx

Set speed to xx.  Speed is divided by the frame rate, lower xx means faster.

Bxx

 Jump to frame xx

Cxx

Skip to next frame and start at row xx
Dxx Halt (xx doesn't do anything)
Exx Set volume (xx = 0 - 15). Obsolete, use the volume column instead!
Fxx Portamento on (slide to new notes), xx = speed
Gxx Portamento off (xx doesn't do anything)
Hxy Trigger hardware sweep up, x - period (0 - 7), y - shift (1 - 7). This feature is only avaliable for the square channels.
Ixy

Trigger hardware sweep down, x - period (0 - 7), y - shift (1 - 7). This feature is only avaliable for the square channels.

Notes: E won't work if an instrument volume effect loop is used. F & G won't work if an instrument frequency effect loop is used. (Pitch, Hi-pitch and Arpeggio)
